# The 2013 Cicada 3301 Puzzle Chain (Second Recruitment)

## Overview

Cicada 3301 ran three annual recruitment puzzles under the PGP-signed name "3301" between 2012 and 2014. The **second puzzle began on/around January 4–5, 2013**, roughly one year to the day after the first. The stated purpose was to find "highly intelligent individuals." Like 2012, each authentic artifact was signed with the same OpenPGP private key to prove it came from 3301 and to let solvers filter out the heavy stream of hoaxes ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cicada_3301)).

A key caveat up front: the **early steps of the 2013 puzzle are well documented** (initial image, OutGuess, Crowley book code, ISO, Twitter hex, the audio file, runic Gematria table, onion sites, telnet). The **later/private steps are much thinner**, told mainly through first-person accounts (notably Marcus Wanner and the "Nox Populi"/"Nox" YouTube solver) rather than recoverable artifacts. The recruitment outcome is partly documented, partly testimonial.

---

## How 2013 Differed From 2012

- **Harder and deeper.** The 2013 chain demanded more cryptography, programming, networking knowledge, and esoteric/literary references than 2012 ([60out](https://www.60out.com/blog/unsolved-mystery-cicada-3301-cypher); [AllThatsInteresting](https://allthatsinteresting.com/cicada-3301)).
- **New occult/literary backbone.** 2013 leaned on **Aleister Crowley's *The Book of the Law* (Liber AL vel Legis)** as a book-cipher key, embedded clues in **original music and imagery**, and introduced the **runic alphabet ("Gematria Primus")** that would become central to 3301 lore ([AllThatsInteresting](https://allthatsinteresting.com/cicada-3301); [Uncovering Cicada Wiki: Gematria Primus](https://uncovering-cicada.fandom.com/wiki/Gematria_Primus)).
- **Anti-collaboration / individualized phases.** Unlike 2012, later 2013 stages were reportedly made **unique per solver** so participants couldn't simply pool answers, enforcing the "go it alone" ethos (discussed below) ([versus.com](https://versus.com/en/news/cicada-3301-the-internet-s-greatest-unsolved-puzzle)).
- **Built as a superset / continuation.** Reporting describes 2013 (and 2014) as building on the prior year, with some 2012 winners pulled in to help run/extend it ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cicada_3301)).

## The Documented Chain (step by step)

**1. The initial image + OutGuess (steganography).** A plain image appeared on 4chan (/x/ and /b/) on ~Jan 4–5, 2013. Running it through **OutGuess** (the same stego tool as 2012) yielded a PGP-signed riddle ([connortumbleson](https://connortumbleson.com/2021/01/25/the-cicada-3301-mystery-puzzle-2/)):
> "A book whose study is forbidden / Once dictated to a beast; / To be read once and then destroyed / Or you shall have no peace."

**2. The Crowley book cipher.** The "book dictated to a beast" was identified as **Aleister Crowley's *The Book of the Law***. A Chapter:Line:Letter coordinate scheme decoded a **Dropbox URL** to a ~130 MB bootable **Linux ISO** ([connortumbleson](https://connortumbleson.com/2021/01/25/the-cicada-3301-mystery-puzzle-2/)).

**3. The ISO and the Twitter feed.** The ISO displayed **"The key is all around you"** and pointed to a Twitter handle **@1231507051321**. Dumping that account's tweets produced a long **hexadecimal** string, converted back to binary (e.g. `xxd -c 65 -r`) ([connortumbleson](https://connortumbleson.com/2021/01/25/the-cicada-3301-mystery-puzzle-2/)).

**4. "The Instar Emergence" (the audio file).** The ISO contained an MP3 named **`761.mp3`** (761 is prime; the track is ~167 seconds, also prime). ID3 tags: title **"The Instar Emergence,"** artist **"3301."** It hid a poem ([Internet Archive](https://archive.org/details/cicada-761); [Uncovering Cicada Wiki](https://uncovering-cicada.fandom.com/wiki/Instar_emergence_(mp3_and_hidden_poem))):
> "Parable 1,595,277,641 / Like the instar, tunneling to the surface / We must shed our own circumferences; / Find the divinity within and emerge."

The "instar" (a cicada developmental stage) and "emergence" are the year's central metaphor. The community notes "The Instar Emergence" sums to **761** via Gematria (matching the filename), and the track features a reversed/distorted guitar that was exhaustively checked for hidden reversed messages — **none confirmed found** ([SoundCloud/NIMW](https://soundcloud.com/nimww/cicada-3301-the-instar-emergence)).

**5. XOR → the runes (Gematria Primus).** **XORing the Twitter-derived binary against `761.mp3`** produced a JPEG: **"Gematria Primus,"** a table of **29 runes**, each mapping to an English letter/sound and to a **prime number** (consecutive primes ascending). This is the **first appearance of the runic alphabet** that later dominates the unsolved **Liber Primus** ([connortumbleson](https://connortumbleson.com/2021/01/25/the-cicada-3301-mystery-puzzle-2/); [Uncovering Cicada Wiki: Gematria Primus](https://uncovering-cicada.fandom.com/wiki/Gematria_Primus)).

**6. OutGuess again → first onion.** Running OutGuess on the Gematria image (whitespace/tab-encoded payload) revealed ([connortumbleson](https://connortumbleson.com/2021/01/25/the-cicada-3301-mystery-puzzle-2/)):
> "Come to **emiwp4muu2ktwknf.onion** … We shall await you there."

**7. Telnet — "web browsers are useless."** The onion site signaled that a normal browser was the wrong tool; the answer was the **dated telnet protocol** to a TCP server hidden service at `emiwp4muu2ktwknf.onion`. Sending the command **`hello`** returned hex that decoded to the next onion, `xsxnaksict6egxkq.onion` ([Uncovering Cicada Wiki: The Telnet Prompt](https://uncovering-cicada.fandom.com/wiki/The_Telnet_Prompt); [connortumbleson](https://connortumbleson.com/2021/01/25/the-cicada-3301-mystery-puzzle-2/)).

**8. ICMP / "knock on the sky."** A hint — *"Sometimes one must knock on the sky and listen to the sound"* — pointed to **pinging (ICMP echo)** the server, which returned unusual ICMP payloads. Combined and decoded, they yielded a **gzip** blob containing another onion (`pklmx2eeh6fjt7zf.onion`) ([connortumbleson](https://connortumbleson.com/2021/01/25/the-cicada-3301-mystery-puzzle-2/)).

**9. GPS posters + phone numbers (physical world).** That onion listed **seven GPS coordinates** worldwide — reported as **Dallas, Okinawa, Moscow, Little Rock, Annapolis, Portland, and Columbus**. Each location had a **physical poster** (with the cicada logo/QR code) bearing a **phone number and access code**. Using the Gematria rune values mapped onto a phone keypad, callers reached **automated recorded messages** giving dataset references and hex offsets ([connortumbleson](https://connortumbleson.com/2021/01/25/the-cicada-3301-mystery-puzzle-2/); QR/telephone-pole detail corroborated by [AllThatsInteresting](https://allthatsinteresting.com/cicada-3301)).

**10. XOR against ISO segments → more onions.** The phone-derived data was XORed against specific ISO file segments (e.g. `560.13`, `560.17`) to produce further onion addresses (e.g. `4l6uipnstbggwjyv.onion`) ([connortumbleson](https://connortumbleson.com/2021/01/25/the-cicada-3301-mystery-puzzle-2/)).

**11. Shamir's Secret Sharing.** Several onions held **SSSS shares** (formatted `##-hexstring`). Collecting the threshold (reported as **5 of 10**) and running **`ssss-combine`** reconstructed a final onion: `p7amjopgric7dfdi.onion` ([connortumbleson](https://connortumbleson.com/2021/01/25/the-cicada-3301-mystery-puzzle-2/)).

**12. Magic squares + final test.** Solvers were also tasked with **magic squares** and, in some stage, asked to **stand up their own Tor hidden service accepting CGI uploads** to submit answers. The final onion presented a **timed quiz (~20 questions)** drawing on the puzzle's literary/math/philosophy references; those who passed could **submit an email address**. Closing message: **"Good luck. 3301"** ([connortumbleson](https://connortumbleson.com/2021/01/25/the-cicada-3301-mystery-puzzle-2/)).

## The Duck / OutGuess "out-guess" Reference

A recurring 3301 motif (across years, prominently associated with the puzzles): a decoded clue led to an **Imgur image of a duck**, which was a **decoy/pun**. Solvers read the words **"out" + "guess"** ("you'd be better off if you 'out-guessed' me," etc.) as the instruction to run images through the **OutGuess** stego tool ([AllThatsInteresting](https://allthatsinteresting.com/cicada-3301)). This is documented Cicada lore; the duck pun is most famously tied to the OutGuess instruction generally rather than uniquely to a single confirmed 2013 step — treat the exact placement within 2013 as **loosely sourced**.

## The "Do Not Break the Puzzle" / Anti-Collaboration Theme

A defining ideological element of 2013 was 3301 actively discouraging crowd-solving and "leeching." The most-cited verbatim warning is ([versus.com](https://versus.com/en/news/cicada-3301-the-internet-s-greatest-unsolved-puzzle); [zmescience](https://www.zmescience.com/feature-post/cicada-3301-puzzle-brightest-minds-posted-unknown-mysterious-organizationt/)):
> "You've shared too much to this point. **We want the best, not the followers.**"

To enforce this, later stages were reportedly **personalized per solver** so shared answers wouldn't work, pushing each finalist to "go it alone" ([versus.com](https://versus.com/en/news/cicada-3301-the-internet-s-greatest-unsolved-puzzle)).

*Note on phrasing:* The exact slogan **"do not break the puzzle"** and explicit "no data-mining / no teams" wording is **not cleanly attested verbatim** in the sources I found for 2013. The documented sentiment is the "we want the best, not the followers" line plus the per-user uniqueness. Treat the precise "do not break the puzzle" wording as **paraphrase/lore unless a primary artifact is produced.**

## What the 2013 Puzzle Led To (Recruitment) — Documented vs. Testimonial

**Documented / consistently reported:**
- The first 2013 puzzle was solved by **Marcus Wanner** (a teenager who had also been a 2012 winner) ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cicada_3301)).
- Solvers who submitted an email received a **questionnaire / ideological screening** asking, in substance: *Do you believe every human has a right to privacy and anonymity? Do you believe information should be free? Do you believe censorship harms humanity?* ([Uncovering Cicada Wiki: The 2013 Winners Questions](https://uncovering-cicada.fandom.com/wiki/The_2013_Winners_Questions); [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cicada_3301)).
- Those who answered satisfactorily were **invited to a private forum** and asked to **build a project advancing the group's ideals** (information freedom, privacy, anti-censorship) ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cicada_3301)).
- 3301 self-described as something **"much like a think tank"** focused on **liberty, privacy, and security** ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cicada_3301)).

**Testimonial (Wanner-sourced, plausible but not independently artifact-verified):**
- Winners were reportedly put in contact and tasked to co-develop cryptographic software. The named project was **CAKES — Cicada Anonymous Key Escrow System**, intended to **protect whistleblowers/users** from retribution ([The Face](https://theface.com/life/cicada-3301-the-codebreakers-still-working-on-the-internets-strangest-mystery)).
- Collaboration **fell apart**: members lost momentum, the project produced little, and **3301 went silent** without warning — timing that overlapped the **Edward Snowden/NSA leaks**, which is cited as one speculated reason for the disappearance ([The Face](https://theface.com/life/cicada-3301-the-codebreakers-still-working-on-the-internets-strangest-mystery)).

## Claims I Could NOT Verify (Flag as Rumor / Likely Conflation)

- **"Tarahumara" and "number stations" as 2013 elements:** I found **no documentation** tying the Tarahumara (the Mexican long-distance-running people) or shortwave **number stations** to the **2013** chain. The 2013 "telnet/ICMP" audio-and-server steps may be **conflated** in popular retellings with number-station aesthetics, and Tarahumara appears to be **misattributed lore**. Treat both as **unverified for 2013.**
- **MIDI specifically:** 2013 used **original music** ("The Instar Emergence" MP3); a distinct **MIDI** artifact in 2013 is **not confirmed** in the sources here. The strong, documented audio artifact is the MP3, not a MIDI file. **Unverified.**
- **"The Warning" 4chan post** (a poster claiming to be an ex-3301 insider calling it a cult/"religion disguised as a progressive scientific organization," urging people to stay away) is **real as a posted message** but its **authenticity/authorship is unverified** and widely regarded as **unsubstantiated** ([as surfaced in search; original is an anonymous 4chan/Pastebin artifact, not PGP-signed by 3301]). Treat as **rumor.**
- **Exact onion addresses and the precise 5-of-10 SSSS / 7-coordinate counts:** these come from community write-ups (connortumbleson, wikis) reconstructing the chain years later; directionally reliable but **specific values may contain reconstruction errors.**
- **Who 3301 actually is** (intelligence agency, bank, private collective, ARG group, individual) remains **completely unknown** — no group has ever credibly claimed it, and all attributions are **speculation** ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cicada_3301)).

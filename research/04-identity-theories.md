# WHO Created Cicada 3301 — Credible Theories and the Evidence

**Bottom line up front:** As of the last verified Cicada 3301 communication (an OpenPGP-signed message in **April 2017**), the identity of the creator(s) of Cicada 3301 is **unknown and unproven**. No individual or organization has ever produced cryptographic proof of authorship (i.e., control of the canonical 3301 PGP private key) and simultaneously been independently corroborated. Everything below is therefore a hypothesis of varying credibility, and the field is, as you noted, heavily polluted with self-promotion, hoaxes, and litigation. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cicada_3301))

## Documented facts (the firm ground)

- A black-and-white image signed **"3301"** appeared on 4chan's /b/ on **January 4–5, 2012**, reading "We are looking for highly intelligent individuals." Three annual puzzle waves followed (2012, 2013, 2014), plus the still-largely-undeciphered runic book **Liber Primus**. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cicada_3301); [Rolling Stone](https://www.rollingstone.com/culture/culture-news/cicada-solving-the-webs-deepest-mystery-84394/))
- Authentic Cicada messages were **OpenPGP-signed with a consistent key**, which is the only reliable authentication anyone has. Many later "Cicada" claims are unsigned and therefore unverifiable. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cicada_3301))
- The group **issued signed denials**: in 2012 it denied illegal hacking (re: Chilean police allegations); in **July 2015** it disavowed an impostor "Cicada" tied to a Planned Parenthood hack, stating "we are not associated with this group in any way." ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cicada_3301))
- **Marcus Wanner** is the best-documented real participant: he and a friend ("Tekk"/Tekknolagi) solved an early puzzle, were vetted on views about **information freedom, online privacy, and opposition to censorship**, and were admitted to a private forum to work on projects. Wanner has said publicly he believes Cicada was a **sophisticated private/hacker collective, not a government or corporation**. ([Rolling Stone](https://www.rollingstone.com/culture/culture-news/cicada-solving-the-webs-deepest-mystery-84394/); [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cicada_3301))
- The puzzles' recurring values — privacy, anonymity, Tor, PGP, "free information," self-overcoming ("shed our own circumferences… emerge"), and the **Instar Emergence** motif — are documented in the artifacts themselves. ([Uncovering Cicada Wiki – Instar emergence](https://uncovering-cicada.fandom.com/wiki/Instar_emergence_(mp3_and_hidden_poem)); [Gematria Primus](https://uncovering-cicada.fandom.com/wiki/Gematria_Primus))

## (a) Intelligence agency / recruitment front (NSA, GCHQ, CIA, MI6, Mossad)

**The theory:** The puzzles test exactly the skills agencies recruit for — advanced cryptography, steganography, programming, OSINT, operational security — so it's a covert recruiting funnel. This is the single most-repeated popular theory. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cicada_3301); [All That's Interesting](https://allthatsinteresting.com/cicada-3301))

**Evidence for:** Only circumstantial — the difficulty and skill profile match agency needs.

**Evidence against (this is the well-supported side):**
- Authentic 3301 communications described an **"international group… unaffiliated with any government,"** which is the opposite of a single-nation recruiter. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cicada_3301))
- Solvers were vetted for **anti-censorship / pro-privacy / anti-surveillance** values — ideologically awkward for the NSA/GCHQ, the world's largest surveillance agencies. ([Rolling Stone](https://www.rollingstone.com/culture/culture-news/cicada-solving-the-webs-deepest-mystery-84394/))
- Real agencies recruit cryptographers through **named, public channels** (university programs, GCHQ's own openly-publicized puzzles), not anonymous global ARGs that send winners to a Welsh phone-booth and physical posters worldwide.
- Press accounts report agency dismissals (commonly summarized as "definitely not us"); treat the exact wording as **secondary/uncorroborated** since no primary on-record agency statement is well-documented. ([All That's Interesting](https://allthatsinteresting.com/cicada-3301))

**Verdict:** Popular but weakly supported; the privacy/anti-surveillance ideology actively cuts against it. Plausible an agency *watched* the talent pool; little to suggest it *ran* the operation.

## (b) A cryptography/privacy collective or "cypherpunk" think-tank

**The theory:** A private group of like-minded technologists promoting privacy, cryptography, anonymity, and free information — essentially heirs to the 1990s **cypherpunk / crypto-anarchy** movement.

**Evidence for (this is the best-supported hypothesis):**
- The vetting questions and the artifacts' stated aims map directly onto cypherpunk values: liberty, privacy, security, anti-censorship, mastery of **PGP and Tor**. Secondary analysis explicitly frames Cicada as sharing cypherpunk ideals and tools. ([Versus / "internet's greatest unsolved puzzle"](https://versus.com/en/news/cicada-3301-the-internet-s-greatest-unsolved-puzzle); [Rolling Stone](https://www.rollingstone.com/culture/culture-news/cicada-solving-the-webs-deepest-mystery-84394/))
- The insider with the most credibility (**Wanner**) concluded it was a private collective built "the same way… free-software developers would," producing privacy/security software. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cicada_3301))
- The self-description as an unaffiliated international group fits a decentralized collective better than any institution. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cicada_3301))

**Evidence against:** "Collective" is hard to falsify and overlaps with the ARG and cult readings; we still lack any verified roster.

**Verdict:** The **most credible** mainstream hypothesis, consistent with both the artifacts and the one well-documented insider account.

## (c) An ARG / alternate-reality game by a private group or individual

**The theory:** It's an elaborate, possibly artistic ARG.

**Evidence for:** Structurally it behaves like a high-end ARG (layered ciphers, real-world dead drops, narrative arc). ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cicada_3301))

**Evidence against:** Wikipedia notes a key disqualifier for a *commercial* ARG: **no monetization or marketing payoff** ever materialized — classic ARGs sell or promote something. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cicada_3301))

**Verdict:** Plausible as a *form*; the "private group/individual recruitment-and-philosophy project" framing overlaps heavily with (b). Not mutually exclusive.

## (d) A cult or quasi-religious / esoteric group

**The theory:** The mystical tone — **Instar Emergence** (insect-metamorphosis-as-enlightenment), the runic **Liber Primus**, **Gematria Primus** (29 runes valued by ascending primes), and recurrent Gnostic/Kabbalistic/sacred-geometry motifs ("find the divinity within and emerge") — suggests an initiatory or quasi-religious order. ([Uncovering Cicada Wiki – Instar emergence](https://uncovering-cicada.fandom.com/wiki/Instar_emergence_(mp3_and_hidden_poem)); [Gematria Primus](https://uncovering-cicada.fandom.com/wiki/Gematria_Primus))

**Evidence for:** The texts are genuinely steeped in Gnostic/"cyber-gnosis" themes; an academic paper, *"The Mystery of Cicada 3301: Constructing Gnosis in Cyberspace,"* analyzes Cicada as framing **gnosis (enlightenment) within libertarian privacy ideals** — i.e., the spiritual language is real, even if the "cult" label is interpretive. ([Academia.edu – Constructing Gnosis in Cyberspace](https://www.academia.edu/37375348/The_Mystery_of_Cicada_3301_Constructing_Gnosis_in_Cyberspace))

**Evidence against:** No documented worship, leader-veneration, recruitment-into-belief, or financial/control structure of an actual cult. The mysticism reads more as **aesthetic/philosophical framing** for a privacy ideology than as a religion.

**Verdict:** "Cult" overstates it; "esoteric/Gnostic-flavored privacy project" is the defensible version. The mystical content is fact; the cult interpretation is speculation.

## (e) Named individuals — accused or self-claimed

This is the most contested and **litigious** area. Handle attributively.

**Marcus Wanner — documented participant, not creator.** Wanner is a real, on-record *solver/insider*, profiled by Rolling Stone, featured in his university paper, and interviewed by outlets. He has **never claimed to have created** Cicada and explicitly described himself as a recruited participant. Treat him as a credible *witness*, not a suspect. ([Rolling Stone](https://www.rollingstone.com/culture/culture-news/cicada-solving-the-webs-deepest-mystery-84394/); [Collegiate Times](https://www.collegiatetimes.com/lifestyle/tech-student-featured-in-rolling-stone/article_d877bf32-abe0-11e4-b7f0-fb83458fe1d2.html))

**Thomas Schoenberger / "Gabriel" — self-associated and accused; heavily disputed and legally sensitive.**
- Schoenberger is a composer/puzzle-maker who **claims a long association** with Cicada — saying it was "a collective that he wrote music for and contributed ideas to from the beginning." He **registered a "Cicada 3301" LLC in 2014**. ([State News](https://statenews.com/article/2025/04/msu-professors-qanon-paper-prompts-lawsuit-with-cicada-3301-puzzle-leader))
- Critics (notably online figure **Laura Dilley** and others) allege he **"gamejacked"** the Cicada name — i.e., appropriated/incorporated it rather than originating it. **Schoenberger denies this.** Whether he was an original creator, a later participant, or an opportunistic claimant is **unresolved and bitterly contested**; there is **no verified PGP-key control** publicly tying him to the original 2012–2014 puzzles. ([State News](https://statenews.com/article/2025/04/msu-professors-qanon-paper-prompts-lawsuit-with-cicada-3301-puzzle-leader))
- **QAnon claim:** MSU professor **Laura Dilley's** 2021 paper *"QAnon Propaganda on Twitter as Information Warfare"* alleged Schoenberger was "involved in early promotion of QAnon narratives" and *speculated* he may have "groomed" Cicada participants into posting as "Q." **Crucially, Dilley herself later characterized this as "an educated guess" rather than a definitive finding.** **Schoenberger categorically denies any QAnon involvement.** He **sued Dilley and MSU** in Michigan's Court of Claims, alleging the paper's methodology was flawed and that it damaged him; the litigation was ongoing/consolidated into 2025–2026. Present the QAnon link as a **contested, self-walked-back allegation under active litigation — not established fact.** ([State News](https://statenews.com/article/2025/04/msu-professors-qanon-paper-prompts-lawsuit-with-cicada-3301-puzzle-leader); [Michigan Court of Claims order, 2025-12-04](https://www.courts.michigan.gov/4982de/siteassets/case-documents/uploads/coc/2026/26-000013-mz/2025-12-04-26-000013-mz-21-order-20251204-order-of-consolidation.pdf))
- Most of the deeper Schoenberger narrative (alleged "disinfo operations," ties to figures like Isaac Kappy, "brotherBox," etc.) circulates on **partisan blogs (Samizdat/therealsamizdat, jimmysllama, threadreader)** that are themselves parties or aligned with parties to the feuds. **Flag all of this as unverified, partisan, and litigation-entangled.** Do not treat it as documented fact. ([Samizdat](https://therealsamizdat.com/2019/09/12/cicada-files-schoenberger-1/); [jimmysllama](https://jimmysllama.com/2021/11/14/19745/))

**Verdict on (e):** No named individual has **proven** authorship of the original Cicada 3301. Wanner is a verified participant who disclaims creator status. Schoenberger is the most prominent **self-associated and accused** figure, but his exact role — founder, later collaborator, or name-appropriator — is **unproven and the subject of active, contested litigation**, and the QAnon link is a speculative, partly-retracted allegation.

## How the hypotheses rank by credibility

1. **Most credible:** A **private privacy/crypto collective in the cypherpunk tradition** (b) — best fit to the artifacts and to Wanner's firsthand account. The ARG (c) and esoteric/Gnostic (d) readings are largely *facets* of this same project rather than rival answers.
2. **Weakly supported:** A single-nation **intelligence-agency front** (a) — popular but contradicted by the group's self-description and anti-surveillance vetting.
3. **Unproven / contested:** Any **named creator** (e). No cryptographically verified attribution exists.
